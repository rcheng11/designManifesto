Ron's Design Manifesto

Rule #1.
Use visual hierarchies

Always know what you want the user to see!
Use size and color hierarchies to guide the user to the most important things on your page. The items you want to pop out should be BIGGER and have a COLOR that STANDS OUT.

Rule #2.
Keep things simple

Limit your color palette (to 1-4 colors) and your fonts (to 1-2 typefaces). By keeping things simpler, you reduce the risk of confusing the viewer, or having typefaces and colors clash.

Rule #3.
Ensure readability

Make sure it's easy to read the text on your website. Harsh and low contrast colors cause eye strain. A lack of whitespace, padding, and properly sized text makes it hard to follow.
